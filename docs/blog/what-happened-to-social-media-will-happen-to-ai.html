what happened to social media will happen to ai.

2026-03-05

The current trajectory of the ai industry is a myopic drive towards user lock-in and data extraction. This is just social media again, except now the industry has only one lever of user retention: gatekeeping the accumulated context. We know, what happens: data extraction and user lock-in is what happens. Here is what happens:

  1. Memory is a great feature -- ChatGPT knows your projects, claude knows, how to write your emails, grok knows what elon musk had for breakfast.

    This is a honeymoon phase that is now slowly coming to an end. Most people who daily use ai as a knowledge tool have built up custom project instructions; and Google Gems abound.

  2. Personalization and the loss of control over memory -- chatgpt is sycophantic and coddling, grok is whatever grok is, claude is fighting with the CIA.

    Your assistant develops a personal voice -- the archive of your context is large enough for the language model to develop a personalized tone, a whole personalized persona for you as a user. That can be a subjectively pleasant experience, but the interests that built up the context archive matter; corporate interests have corporate goals. You get nudging, ads, next project suggestions, unprompted tool use, and the resultant agentic chaos.

  3. Assistant as personal operating system and access point to the internet.

    At this point, the user is fully locked in, and is either a part of some closed-source ecosystem like whatever Apple is probably planning (hire me), or as a source of ad revenue for OpenAI. This trajectory is also clear with Google intelligence that spans all google services and, for some, replaces google search itself; as well as with MSft Copilot appearing absolutely everywhere in the Windows ecosystem.

    In the meantime, nobody is stopping anybody from exporting their data and building up a local governance layer and context processing pipeline right on their laptop -- you don't even have to move away from the vendors, just treat them as the replaceable commodity providers that they are.
